  4. Communication complexity: Moderate to High; they need to explain medical procedures, provide instructions, and navigate sensitive conversations.

  2. Emotional intelligence required: High; handling patients with empathy, understanding, and patience—often under stressful conditions.
  3. Reading social cues: Essential; understanding patients' non-verbal signs of discomfort or distress is crucial.
  4. Level of relationship building needed: Moderate to High; building trust and rapport with patients and team members is essential.
  5. Depth of interpersonal understanding required: High; recognizing individual patient needs and adjusting care accordingly.

  1. Cultural sensitivity requirements: High; patients come from diverse backgrounds and exhibit varied cultural needs and preferences which must be respectfully navigated.
  2. Conflict resolution needs: Moderate; occasional conflicts may arise from patient or family dissatisfaction or team dynamics that need addressing.
  3. Leadership/influence components: Low to Moderate; although not primary leaders, they need to assert influence in patient care situations.
  4. Need for empathy and emotional support: Very High; providing comfort and reassurance to patients and families.
  5. Real-time social adaptation requirements: High; must adapt communication and care techniques in real-time based on patient behavior and feedback.

  3. Are the interactions predictable or highly variable?: Highly variable; each patient's condition and needs differ greatly.
  4. Does the role require genuine human connection?: Absolutely; human connection is vital for effective care and support.
