  • Moderate emotional engagement needed, mostly in terms of teamwork and collaborating with other scientists on research projects.

  1. Does it require reading social cues?
  • Minimal to moderate need for reading social cues, mostly in occasional team settings or meetings.

  • Building professional relationships is important but not deeply personal; primarily focused on professional and academic interactions.
  1. Depth of interpersonal understanding required:
  • Moderate depth required, mainly to ensure effective project collaboration and communication within teams.

  1. Conflict Resolution Needs:
  • Might infrequently arise in research teams, requiring some level of conflict resolution skills.
  1. Leadership/Influence Components:
  • Essential for senior roles in leading projects and teams, mentoring junior researchers, and influencing the direction of research.
  1. Need for Empathy and Emotional Support:
  • Moderate need when working in teams and mentoring, especially to foster a supportive research environment.
  1. Real-Time Social Adaptation Requirements:
  • Limited to periodic real-time team interactions and meetings.

Step 4: Assess AI Replaceability

  1. Can AI simulate these interactions effectively?
  • AI can handle data analysis, simulation, and some report generation but struggles with nuanced collaboration and complex problem-solving discussions.
  1. How much nuanced human understanding is needed?
  • A notable amount is needed for collaboration, hypothesis generation, and peer review processes.
  1. Are the interactions predictable or highly variable?
  • Somewhat predictable in patterns but can vary significantly in collaborative scenarios and hypothesis testing.
  1. Does the role require genuine human connection?
  • To a degree, particularly in collaborative and mentoring roles, but less so than in customer-facing roles.
